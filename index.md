## Welcome! Bem-vindo! Benvenuto! Wilkommen!

I am a PhD Researcher in Transnational Governance at [Scuola Normale Superiore and Scuola Superiore Sant'Anna](https://www.sns.it/en/persona/marcel-artioli), Florence. With over a decade of experience in agrarian and rural development, I served as State Coordinator for Brazil’s National Land Credit Program (PNCF) at the State of São Paulo and as an analyst for the São Paulo Land Institute (ITESP). My academic expertise in International Political Economy (IPE) and environmental policy is supported by a master’s in international relations from the Graduate Program in International Relations San Tiago Dantas (Unesp, Unicamp, PUC-SP), Brazil, and a bachelor degree in International Relations from São Paulo State University (Unesp). 

My PhD research investigates the roles of state and non-state actors in steering environmental policies in Brazil. The study explores the dynamics of transnational climate change governance, examining the actions of various actors and their partnerships in addressing climate change and other environmental issues. My research interests are critical political economy, global environmental politics, critical environmental politics, political ecology, transnational climate change governance, and Brazilian environmental policies. 

My research career began at São Paulo State University (2016–2019), where I worked with the Observatório de Regionalismo (ODR) and the Center for International Studies and Analysis (NEAI). Since then, I have expanded my international profile through the German Institute of Development and Sustainability (IDOS), first as a 2022 Managing Global Governance (MGG) Academy Fellow and most recently as a Visiting Guest PhD Researcher in the Environmental Governance Department (2025). I am also an active member of the European Consortium for Political Research (ECPR)
